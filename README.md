## Building now

**Sentience** — An open strategy and prototype for the trust layer of autonomous
vehicles. Public trust in AVs has stayed flat for five years while the safety record
improved, and I think the cause is architectural: the driving stack knows what it
perceives, intends, and feels, and none of it reaches the cabin. Sentience proposes the
missing signals as a COVESA VSS extension and ships a browser studio where anyone can
author cabin experiences with zero access to a car. Research in progress; the paper,
strategy report, and prototype repo are coming soon.

**HealthSentinel** — A consumer health app built solo with Claude Code. Scan a product
barcode and it parses the ingredients, then flags anything banned or recalled in the US
or EU. In private testing ahead of launch; repo goes public at release.

## How I work

Write the strategy, build the prototype, publish both, and let the working version make
the argument. The cost of proving a concept has collapsed, and that changes what a
product manager can be. A working demo converts a skeptical room faster than any
document, and this account applies that method in the open.
